The Founder

His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Srila Prabhupada was a spiritual leader and the founder Acarya of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), also known as the Hare Krishna movement. He appeared (born) in Calcutta, India in 1896, and spent most of his life spreading the teachings of the Bhagavad-gita “As it is” and the message of Lord Kṛṣṇa throughout the globe.

What is ISKCON?

The International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), known colloquially as the Hare Krishna movement or Hare Krishnas, is a Gaudiya Vaishnava Hindu religious organization. ISKCON was founded in 1966 by His Divine Grace A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada. Its core beliefs are based on Hindu scriptures, particularly the Bhagavad Gita and the Bhāgavata Purana. ISKCON is "the largest and, arguably, most important branch" of Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition, which has had adherents in India since the early 16th century and American and European devotees since the early 1900s. ISKCON was formed to spread the practice of Bhakti yoga, the practice of love of God in which those involved (bhaktas) dedicate their thoughts and actions towards pleasing Lord Sri Krishna, who has been confirmed as the Supreme Personality of Godhead in the Bhagavad Gita and other Scriptures.

Activities

Chanting

Chanting is a rhythmical repetition (silently or aloud) of a song, prayer, or sound. It is one of the most ancient spiritual practices and a part of spiritual paths. The devotees here chant the ‘Hare Kṛṣṇa’ Mahā-mantra with a minimum of 16 rounds a day.

Bhagavad-Gita Classes

ISKCON provides the Bhagavad Gita classes at it’s temple so that everyone can understand Bhagavad Gita and learn how to apply it’s teachings in their daily life. These classes makes a person spiritually advanced to achieve the higher goals of life.

Kirtan

Kirtan, a form of devotional call-and-response chanting first popularized five hundred years ago in India by Sri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu, is practiced regularly by ISKCON devotees and has now spread around the world by efforts of the Vaishnava community.

Book Distribution

The devotees come out of the temple with books in their hands, to distribute this knowledge for the benefit of the society by going house to house, temples and streets, trying to give knowledge to people.
